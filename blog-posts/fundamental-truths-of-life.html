Fundamental Truths I Had to Learn to Stop Feeling Behind at 24

Published on: December 7, 2025

Home

There’s a specific kind of anxiety that doesn’t come from failure, but from awareness.

You look around and feel late.
Late to skills.
Late to credibility.
Late to momentum.

At 24, I started feeling like I was already lagging behind.
Not because I had done nothing, but because I could finally see the game and didn’t like my position in it.

Writing this was my way of stepping back and identifying a few fundamentals that cut through that noise.

These aren’t motivational ideas.
They’re structural truths about how life actually works.

1. Being alive is the biggest unfair advantage

The most underestimated fact about life is simply being alive, followed closely by being healthy.

We tend to compare ourselves horizontally with peers, but forget the vertical comparison:
the people who had enormous potential, high performance, even early promise, and never reached this age.

You don’t need gratitude here.
Just accuracy.

Dead people don’t feel behind.

2. You don’t choose the cards, you choose how well you play them

In any real game, players don’t get to pick their starting hand.

Some are dealt time.
Some capital.
Some early clarity.
Others uncertainty, late starts, or wasted years.

The players who consistently deliver high quality performance regardless of the cards are the ones who eventually become valuable.

Life isn’t a set of isolated sub games.
Life itself is the game.

Lost time, missed opportunities, lack of ideal skills or resources, these are just part of the hand you’re dealt.

At some point, the only move left is to stop negotiating with the deck and demonstrate performance.

3. Every position comes with tradeoffs

New entrants in an industry often lack the ability to make fast, high quality decisions under pressure the way seasoned experts can.

But seasoned experts often lose the ability to see problems freshly, from new angles and first principles.

There is no position that’s objectively superior.
Only different tradeoffs.

The mistake is assuming that not being in one position yet makes you worse, rather than simply different.

4. Life is non-linear, whether you like it or not

We secretly expect progress to be linear:
put in input, get proportional output.

Real life doesn’t work that way.

Outcomes are often the result of sudden shifts, inflection points, and compounding effects, where small events can create disproportionately large consequences.

If progress isn’t linear, then comparison by age, timeline, or checkpoint is fundamentally flawed.

Late movement doesn’t mean late arrival.

5. You are not defined by your past

A 40 year old who has never touched a paintbrush can still create something remarkable.

A fool can become wise.
A coward courageous.
Someone who looked like a “late starter” can become a great leader.

The past constrains you, but it doesn’t lock you out.

You can choose a harder path later and still become extraordinary.
The only thing you can’t do is wait indefinitely and expect outcomes to change.

Closing thought

Feeling behind isn’t always a signal of failure.

Sometimes it’s the moment you finally understand the rules,
right before you start playing seriously.
